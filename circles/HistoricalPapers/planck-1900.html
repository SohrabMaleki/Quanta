M. Planck (1900) - On the Theory of the Energy Distribution Law of the Normal Spectrum

09 March, 2025

Abstract

At the end of the nineteenth century and the beginning of the twentieth, discrepancies were observed between experimental results and theoretical predictions based on classical physics. One such inconsistency was the problem of black-body radiation. While the total radiative power of a black body could be described using thermodynamic considerations, the frequency distribution of that radiation conflicted with theoretical expectations. In 1900, Max Planck proposed a hypothesis that required energy to be quantized, offering a solution for the frequency distribution of black-body radiation that agreed closely with experimental data. This achievement is regarded as a cornerstone of quantum physics.
