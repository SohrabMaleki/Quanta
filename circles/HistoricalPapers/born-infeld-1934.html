M. Born & L. Infeld (1934) - Foundations of the New Field Theory

10 June, 2025

Abstract

Born–Infeld electrodynamics is a nonlinear modification of Maxwell’s theory of electromagnetism proposed in the 1930s by Max Born and Leopold Infeld. Its main motivation was to remove the singularities (infinite self-energy) of point charges, such as the infinite energy associated with the electric field of an electron in classical Maxwell theory. The Born–Infeld model introduces an upper bound on the electromagnetic field strength, leading to finite self-energy for point charges. This theory is consistent with special relativity and has reappeared in modern physics, particularly in string theory, where the Born–Infeld action naturally arises in the description of D-branes.

In this session, we will review this paper by Max Born and Leopold Infeld, in which a model of nonlinear electrodynamics is introduced to resolve the singularity problem of point charges. This model, inspired by the idea of an upper limit on velocity in special relativity, imposes an upper bound on electric and magnetic fields. Next, we will examine several issues within the framework of this model, and finally, we will point out the effective role of this model in some aspects of string theory.

Lecturer: Alireza Talebi
