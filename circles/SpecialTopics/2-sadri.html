Quasi Inversion of Quantum & Classical Channels in Finite Dimensions

10 August, 2025

Abstract

In closed quantum systems, the evolution of states under the Schrödinger equation is fully reversible — meaning one can return exactly to the past. However, if the system is open and interacts with its surroundings, then without complete control over the environment, exact reversal becomes impossible. How are these non-unitary evolutions, also known as “quantum channels,” described? If we cannot completely eliminate environmental noise, to what extent can we bring the system “close” to its initial state again? And more importantly, what answers does the classical version of these questions offer, and what similarities or differences emerge compared to the quantum case? In this week’s “Quanta,” I will present a brief overview of my and my colleagues’ efforts to find answers to these questions.

Lecturer: Koorosh Sadri

Physics PhD Student @ Penn. State University
