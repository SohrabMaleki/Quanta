Scaling Limits: Effective Dynamics Vs. Dynamical Mean-Field Theory

05 July, 2025

Abstract

Stochastic Gradient Descent (SGD) is a key algorithm for training neural networks, yet its success in complex, high-dimensional settings is not fully understood. Tools from the statistical physics of disordered systems (spin glasses), especially Dynamical Mean-Field Theory (DMFT), offer insight by approximating the dynamics of learning. In this thesis, we show that DMFT fails to capture crucial fluctuation effects near saddle points, in what's known as the diffusive regime. There, SGD behaves via a stochastic differential equation (SDE), something DMFT does not account for. This highlights the limits of mean-field approaches and the importance of the effective dynamics using the information exponent in high-dimensional learning.

Lecturer: Parsa Rangriz

Mathematics PhD Student @ UC San Diego
