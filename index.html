About Quanta

Quanta is a student‑led initiative originated at the Sharif University of Technology that organises open study circles to explore the depth and breadth of physics. Our aim is to create a collaborative environment where students and enthusiasts can learn, discuss and teach each other topics ranging from historical breakthroughs to contemporary research.


We host a variety of circles. They're organized into three main classes: Mathematics & Physics, Historical Papers and Special Topics. Each circle features lectures, discussions and resources curated by passionate organisers. Whether you're just starting your physics journey or seeking a deeper understanding, Quanta welcomes you.


Guide on Study Circles

Mathematics & Physics: These set of circles are dedicated to study some well-known topics on physics and mathematics. Their purpose varies from introduction of the topic to others (especially courses we lack at SUT), getting a deeper understanding of the topic, delve more into mathematical details, etc. This is where students discuss and learn a topic together.


Historical Papers: These sessions are dedicated to read and review the groundbreaking, usually old papers from great minds of physics in the history. The aim is understanding the idea, environment and the paradigm of their thinking which has led to these breakthrough discoveries.


Special Topics: These sessions are dedicated to little-known, relatively advanced topics and modern papers that worth investigating together.
